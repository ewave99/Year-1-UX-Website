Sausages with Apple Mash

Ingredients

  • 700g floury potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 8 Cumberland sausages
  • 25g butter
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp plain flour
  • 250ml beef stock
  • 2 apples (about 280g/10oz), peeled and chopped into small chunks
  • 3 tbsp milk

Serves: 4

Prep time: 10 mins

Cooking time: 1 hr

Method

  1. Cook the potatoes in boiling, salted water for 15 mins until tender. In the meantime, heat a non-stick frying pan and add the sausages. Cook over medium heat for 15-20 mins, turning occasionally, until cooked through. Remove the sausages and keep warm.
  2. Add a knob of butter and the onion to the pan, then cook for 15-20 mins until soft. Stir in the flour, then gradually add the beef stock, stirring to make a sauce. Simmer for 2 mins, then pour into a jug and keep warm.
  3. Cook the apples in half of the remaining butter for 5-10 mins until softened. Drain the potatoes, mash well with the rest of the butter and the milk, and fold in the apples. Serve with the sausages and onion gravy.
