Fig & Prosciutto Pizzettas

Ingredients

  • Knob of butter
  • 3 shallots, finely diced
  • 250g full-fat crème fraîche
  • 50g parmesan, finely grated
  • 4 ripe figs, cut into chunks
  • 125g ball good-quality buffalo mozzarella, roughly torn
  • 2 handfuls rocket
  • 6 slices prosciutto, torn into strips
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp good balsamic vinegar
  • For the dough:
  • 400g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 7g sachet fast-action dried yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp olive oil

Serves: 6 - 8

Prep time: 30 mins

Cooking time: 20 mins plus rising

Method

  1. To make the dough, mix the flour, yeast, and salt in a bowl. Measure 250ml hand-warm water, add the oil, then mix into the flour to form a wet dough. Cover with oiled cling film and leave to rise for 1 hr or until doubled in size. In the meantime, heat the butter, soften the shallots, and set aside.
  2. Heat the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7. Tip the dough onto a floured surface, knead briefly to smooth, then divide into 6-8 balls. Roll out each to a thin circle, placing them on floured baking sheets.
  3. Mix the crème fraîche, Parmesan, and shallots, adding a little salt and lots of black pepper. Spread this mixture over the dough and bake for 10-15 mins until the edges are crisp and the pizzettas are slightly puffed.
  4. Toss the figs and mozzarella with rocket. Top the pizzettas with the prosciutto. Mix the oil and vinegar, drizzle over the pizzettas, and serve.
